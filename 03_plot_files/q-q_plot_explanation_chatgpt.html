Q-Q Plot: Assessing Normality of Residuals

A Q-Q (Quantile-Quantile) plot is a graphical tool used to assess whether a dataset follows a particular theoretical distribution. In this case, the Q-Q plot compares the residuals from a regression model to a normal distribution.

Interpreting the Plot:

  • The x-axis represents the theoretical quantiles from a standard normal distribution.
  • The y-axis represents the sample quantiles of the residuals.
  • The red diagonal line represents the expected values if the residuals were perfectly normally distributed.
  • The black dots represent the actual residuals.

Key Observations:

  • Most points fall along the red line, indicating that the residuals are approximately normal.
  • Slight deviations from the line at the tails suggest mild departures from normality.
  • If the residuals followed a perfect normal distribution, all points would lie exactly on the red line.
  • Outliers or heavy tails (points deviating significantly from the line) may indicate skewness or kurtosis in the data.

Overall, this Q-Q plot suggests that the model residuals are fairly normal, though there may be some deviation at the extremes.
