Comparing the Just Value of an Emerald Gardens House to the Model Predictions

You can examine the comparison of the model's predictions to the Property Appraiser's Just Value by selecting a house from the drop-down list. If you want to narrow your selections, you can Filter by Street so that the Select House Address list shows only those houses on that street.

As soon as you select a house, the light blue cards will show you the factors that went into the model's estimation and the Property Appraiser's Just valuation. The cards in the Predicted Values column show :

  • High: mean estimate plus 2 standard deviations.
  • Mean: mean estimate
  • Low: mean estimate minus 2 standard deviations.

Important: I don't know the valuation formula used by the Property Appraiser. My estimations are based solely on the statistical computations of the model. While there are outliers, you will find that the model's estimations are very near the Property Appraiser's valuations.

The upper and lower figures show a band of valuations which encompasses 95% of the variance in the estimation. That means the the the likelihood of the Property Appraiser's figure has a 95% probability of falling somewhere between the high and low figures.

Each January, the Sarasota County Property Appraiser estimates the fair market value of every parcel in the county. In this context, "fair market value" refers to a conservative estimate, typically 5% to 10% lower than the expected sale price of a property.

Another key component in this calculation is the cost of sale, which includes expenses such as broker commissions, title searches, and document preparation. Based on historical litigation, the Property Appraiser applies a 15% reduction to account for these costs.

Example Calculation: If a house sells for $500,000 in an arms-length transaction, the "just value" is computed as follows:

  • Sale Price: $500,000
  • Conservative Estimate (-10%): -$50,000
  • Cost of Sale (-15%): -$75,000
  • Just Value: $375,000
