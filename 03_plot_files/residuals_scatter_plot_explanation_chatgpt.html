Residual Plot: Evaluating Model Fit

A residual plot is used to assess the adequacy of a regression model by visualizing the residuals (differences between observed and predicted values) against the fitted values.

Interpreting the Plot:

  • The x-axis represents the fitted (predicted) values.
  • The y-axis represents the residuals (errors).
  • The black dots represent individual residuals for each observation.
  • The red dashed line is the reference line at zero, indicating no residual error.

Key Observations:

  • Residuals appear to be randomly scattered around the red dashed line, suggesting no obvious pattern.
  • If the residuals exhibit a clear pattern (e.g., curvature or funnel shape), this may indicate model misspecification or heteroscedasticity.
  • The residuals seem to have a roughly constant variance, which supports the assumption of homoscedasticity.
  • A few outliers are present, but they do not appear to dominate the overall trend.

Overall, this residual plot suggests that the model captures the relationship between the predictors and the response variable well, with no major violations of regression assumptions.
